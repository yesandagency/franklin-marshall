' - name: accordion title: 'About the Program' contents: - heading: 'Our Program & Courses' type: 'two-col' description: '

Environmental science is a major and minor at F&M. You will study the environment through a blended lens of biology, chemistry, and geology. This program will prepare you to evaluate and address key environmental problems by helping you develop critical, science-based decision-making skills. Your learning won’t take place in the classroom alone; it’s only fitting to study environmental science by venturing out into the environment itself. You’ll engage in exciting hands-on outdoor learning; dive into your findings in the lab; and embark on independent and collaborative research—all while utilizing F&M’s cutting-edge field, laboratory, and computing equipment.

By the time you graduate, you will:
  • Have developed an understanding of the environment rooted in the fields of biology, chemistry, and geology
  • Understand the interactions between humans and natural environmental systems in the past, present, and future
  • Be able to describe various indicators of environmental change, including global, regional and local change (including climate change); water and air quality; ecosystem resilience; biodiversity; natural resource use; and human population
  • Develop critical, science-based decision-making to help you understand and improve environmental issues such as land use; the conservation of endangered species; habitat degradation; the impacts of global change on animals, plants, and humans; water and energy resources; and pollution
  • Evaluate real-world applications of scientific analysis to study environmental challenges, and understand how such analyses inform policy responses to human-induced environmental change

Curious what environmental studies classes are like? Take a look at our course catalog to see the depth and breadth of what’s available to you.

F&M’s location provides the perfect landscape for studying environmental science. Lancaster County’s flourishing agricultural scene, the nearby Susquehanna River, and F&M’s Millport and Spalding nature conservancies provide exciting learning opportunities outside of the classroom. It’s also not uncommon for students to work alongside their professors and become an integral part of their ongoing research.

Research Opportunities

As you become fully immersed in your environmental science studies, you may find yourself forming theories or questions. The good news? You don’t have to wait until graduate school to investigate. Every student at F&M has extraordinary opportunities to engage in independent or faculty-led research.

Environmental science faculty are expert researchers working on a wide array of environmental topics, including:

  • Wetland and floodplain restoration (Dorothy Merritts and Robert Walter, professors of geosciences)
  • Trace metal cycling in the marine environment (Jennifer Morford, professor of chemistry)
  • Environmental change and extinction in modern and ancient marine environments (Carol de Wet, professor of geosciences)
  • Understanding and predicting responses of aquatic communities and organisms to environmental change (Peter Fields, Janet Fischer, Mark Olson, Joe Thompson, professors of biology)
  • Soil biogeochemistry, carbon cycling and global change (Chris Williams, professor of environmental science)
  • Plant physiology, ecophysiology, and ecology (Sybil Gotsch and Timothy Sipe, associate professors of biology)
  • Organism-environment interactions of plants, birds, and mammals (Daniel Ardia, professor of biology)
  • Environmental applications of remote sensing and Geographic Information Systems (Andrew de Wet, professor of geosciences, and Tim Bechtel, senior teaching professor of geosciences)

Off-Campus Study

Off-campus study, in the U.S. and internationally, is encouraged. Our students have studied at field stations and laboratories in Woods Hole, Ma., the Australian rainforest, and the savanna of Kenya. Learn more about off-campus study.

What happens after you graduate with a degree in environmental science? A plethora of opportunities awaits.

Graduate School

A majority of our graduates go on to graduate school after F&M. Many complete or enroll in doctoral programs in science, medicine or law. Our graduates have completed their advanced degrees at the best universities in the U.S. and abroad.

Career Paths

Career outcomes for environmental science graduates are diverse. Many obtain entry-level positions in the environmental and geotechnical consulting field and as sustainability analysts or consultants. Our alumni have also held positions such as: applied science consultant, associate chemist, attorney, biology teacher, energy industry analyst, environmental analyst, environmental consultant, environmental scientist, geophysicist, global sustainability manager, hydrologist, marine biology researcher, marine chemist, public health researcher, resident naturalist, senior associate geologist, strategic planning analyst, sustainability analyst, sustainability planner, water-quality technician, wolf-monitoring technician.

With a liberal arts degree, the world is at your fingertips. Just keep in mind that environmental scientists can:

  • Work indoors and outdoors
  • Work with plants, animals and ecosystems
  • Work on policy, law and governance
  • Work on environmental remediation and pollution control
  • Work on human health and ecosystem health
  • Evaluate and solve a wide range of technological problems
  • And so much more!
